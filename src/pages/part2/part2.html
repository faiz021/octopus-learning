Abnormal sensitivity to light

Deviations from the normal hill of vision can be used to identify the shape of visual loss.

Constricted boundaries or depressions of sensitivity indicate deviations from normal.

Abnormal sensitivity to light

In normal eyes sensitivity is highest in the central area, gradually dropping towards the periphery - the normal hill of vision.

Pathologies affecting sensitivity to light create deviations (depressions and boundary constrictions) in the hill of vision.

This results in the pathological hill of vision.

The perimetric test

A hemispheric cupola is used to project light stimuli across the visual field.

The stimuli and the background they are projected onto are highly standardised.

The perimetric test

The concave surface of the hemispheric cupola has a standardised uniform white background.

Light stimuli are standardised in terms of shape, size, colour, light intensity and duration.

Round white stimulus, brighter than the background are most commonly used.

The perimetric test

The subject sits in front of the cupola with their head stabilised.

They then fixate on a central target.

Each time a stimulus is seen, the subject presses a response button.

Sensitivity threshold

The subject is shown stimuli of increasing levels of intensity.

When the stimulus reaches a level that the subject can see the intensity level is recorded.

This minimum level defines the subject’s sensitivity to light.

This point between seeing and not seeing is described as the sensitivity threshold.

The decibel scale

Perimetry testing uses the decibel scale.

The unit of measurement in perimetry is therefore the decibel (dB).

The decibel range in perimetry is typically 0 dB to approximately 32 dB at the fovea (in a 20 year old with normal vision).

A sensitivity threshold of 0 dB means a person is unable to see the brightest stimuli.

Summary

Deviations from the normal hill of vision identify the shape of visual loss.

In perimetry testing standardised stimuli are projected in a hemispheric cupola across an individual’s visual field.

Subjects are shown stimuli of increasing intensity at different points to identify their sensitivity threshold.

The unit of measurement in perimetry is the decibel (dB).

Graphic representations of the sensitivity threshold include numerical and grey scale.
