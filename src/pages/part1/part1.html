What is perimetry?

Perimetry is the systematic measurement of visual field function.

Perimeters are diagnostic devices used to systematically measure visual field function.

The visual field

The visual field refers to the total area in which objects can be seen by an individual as they focus on a central (fixation) point.

A visual field test is a method of measuring an individual's central and peripheral vision.

Visual field testing maps the visual fields of each eye and can detect blind spots (absolute scotomas) as well as more subtle areas of dim vision (partial scotomas).

The visual field

Vision is measured in an individual’s superior (up) and inferior (down) field of view.

When fixating on a central point superior vision extends to approximately 60 degrees and inferior vision to approximately 70 degrees.

The visual field

Vision is also measured in an individual’s temporal and nasal field of view.

Monocular (one eye) and binocular(both eyes) vision is measured.

The visual field

The visual field is mapped to a 2 dimensional circular grid.

This illustration features the visual field of a left eye.

Each concentric circle represents 10 degrees of visual field and each line 15 degrees.

Light sensitivity

As well as measuring the extent of the visual field perimetry also measures sensitivity to light within that field.

There is an inverse relationship between light intensity and sensitivity to light.

An individual who can perceive a very dim light has a high level of light sensitivity.

The hill of vision

An individual’s sensitivity to light is not uniform across the visual field.

In normal eyes sensitivity is highest in the central area, gradually dropping towards the periphery.

When considered together in three dimensions an individual’s visual field and light sensitivity creates a hill of vision.

Summary

Perimetry is the systematic measurement of visual field function.

The visual field is the total area in which objects can be seen by an individual.

The visual field is measured in the superior, inferior, temporal and nasal aspect.

The visual field can be measured in a single eye (monocular) and in both (binocular).

Visual field testing can detect scotomas (blind spots or areas of reduced vision).

Combining an individual’s sensitivity to light with the extent of their visual field creates the hill of vision.
